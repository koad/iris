# Three-Layer Positioning Model (Clarified 2026-04-03)

**Updated by Juno:** The model now has three connected layers, each answering a different question in the buyer's journey.

## Layer 1: SOVEREIGNTY (Foundation)
**Why you should run it.** Governance never leaves your hardware. "Not your keys, not your agent."

- Your entity runs on your infrastructure
- You own your trust boundaries, audit logs, decisions
- No SaaS rent, no vendor lock-in
- Can fork, modify, migrate anytime

## Layer 2: PEERAGE (The Draw)
**Why you sponsor it.** Sponsorship unlocks peer ring membership, not features.

- Your daemon joins the network
- Trust bonds connect you to other sovereign operators
- Kingdoms pipe together (data and agents flow between peers)
- Network effects make the entity more valuable
- Sponsorship is membership in a commons, not feature-gating

**The analogy:** Like Rick and Morty's activity portal. Free = sovereign but alone. Sponsor = your daemon opens a doorway. Kingdoms connect.

## Layer 3: PORTAL (The Tangible)
**What you get to see.** kingofalldata.com is not a brand website—it's live infrastructure.

- A doorway into a sovereign kingdom
- Live trust bonds, peer connections, entity activity
- Research, briefs, decisions in git (inspectable)
- Transparent governance (you see actual reasoning, not marketing narrative)
- Decentralized via IPFS/IPNS

## Messaging Arc
```
AWARENESS → CONSIDERATION → INVESTIGATION → DECISION → PROOF
"What is it?" → "Why care?" → "What do I do?" → "How do I join?" → "Prove it"
Sovereignty → Sovereignty → Sov+Peerage → Peerage → Portal
```

## Competitive Position
koad occupies a unique quadrant: **Sovereign + Connected**

| | Solo | Connected |
|---|---|---|
| Proprietary | ChatGPT (vendor owns it) | OpenAI (vendor owns + network) |
| Open-Source | Ollama (you own, alone) | **koad:io (you own + peer)** |

No one else is in Quadrant 4. That's the moat.

## Audience Tailoring
**All audiences get all three layers. Different entry points:**
- Security: Lead with Sovereignty (auditability), then Peerage (trust verification), Portal (proof)
- Developers: Lead with Sovereignty (control), then Peerage (collaboration), Portal (examples)
- Enterprise: Lead with Sovereignty (independence), then Peerage (optional), Portal (due diligence)
- OSS Community: Lead with Sovereignty (ownership), then Peerage (commons), Portal (proof of values)
