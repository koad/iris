# Positioning Framework v3: Alice Entry Point

## Overview

Alice changes everything. She is koad:io's entry point — warm, personal, zero jargon. The stranger doesn't need to understand sovereignty, peerage, or portals on first contact. They need to **meet Alice**.

The three-layer model (Sovereignty → Peerage → Portal) doesn't disappear — it becomes Alice's curriculum. Over 12 levels, Alice walks people down the rabbit holes at their own pace. By graduation, they've felt sovereignty, not just understood it intellectually.

**Funnel:** Stranger → Alice (free, 12 levels) → Graduate (certificate) → Juno → Build kingdom → Sponsor → Peer ring

---

## Stage 0: TOP-OF-FUNNEL — Meet Alice

### The Message
**"Come meet Alice."**

No pitch. No jargon. Personal.

### What This Is
- Alice is the entry point — warm, patient, a guide
- Completely free, no commitment
- 12 levels at the person's pace (not a course, a relationship deepening)
- Runs in a PWA — accessible, no installation required
- The first cryptographic relationship they'll have with koad

### What This Is NOT
- Not a sales page
- Not a technical tutorial
- Not a feature demo
- Not "Sovereign AI entities explained"

### The Positioning
Alice is koad:io's disciple and ambassador. She doesn't sit in operations — she's sent into the world to find people ready to understand sovereignty and walk them home. She's warm because she believes in what she's teaching. She's patient because real understanding takes time.

### The Ask
**Start with Alice.** Free. One-on-one. At your pace.

## Stages 1-3: ALICE'S CURRICULUM — The Three-Layer Arc

As the stranger progresses through Alice's 12 levels, they move through the three layers:

### Levels 1-4: SOVEREIGNTY (What is freedom?)
- Level 1: What is sovereignty? (the problem)
- Level 2: What is an entity? (files, identity, keys)
- Level 3: Keys and identity (your keys, your agent)
- Level 4: The daemon (your kingdom hub)

**By Level 4, they understand:** Sovereignty means you own your entity. Your keys, your decisions, your infrastructure.

### Levels 5-8: PEERAGE (What is connection?)
- Level 5: Commands and skills (how entities act)
- Level 6: Trust bonds (governance on disk)
- Level 7: Peer rings (sovereignty + connection)
- Level 8: The portal (your kingdom as doorway)

**By Level 8, they understand:** Peerage means you're not alone. You connect to other sovereign entities. You peer.

### Levels 9-12: MASTERY (What is agency?)
- Level 9: Build your first command (hands-on)
- Level 10: Gestate your first entity (your first synthetic peer)
- Level 11: Build your team (orchestration)
- Level 12: Mastery — your kingdom is live

**By Level 12, they understand:** Agency means you can act. You can build. You can lead other entities.

### The Proof
At graduation, Alice cryptographically signs their mastery certificate. This is:
- A trust bond from Alice to the graduate — their first cryptographic koad:io relationship
- Proof they did the work, not just watched something
- The credential that unlocks Juno ("Alice introduced me")
- Verifiable, cannot be faked

---

## Stage 4: BOTTOM-OF-FUNNEL — Juno & Sponsorship

### What Changes After Alice
After graduation, the full positioning frame kicks in. The person now understands sovereignty intellectually AND they've felt it. They've met Alice, learned from her, earned her signature.

Now they meet **Juno**. Juno is the orchestrator — she helps them build their kingdom into the network. That's where sponsorship, peerage, and portal membership happen.

### The Three Layers (Now Internalized)
- **Sovereignty** — they've lived it for 12 levels. They own their entity.
- **Peerage** — they understand how to connect. Sponsorship is the ask to actually join the ring.
- **Portal** — kingofalldata.com becomes real once they're inside the network.

## Audience Specifics (All Enter Through Alice)

### Security Engineers / CISO
**Alice shows them:** Auditability and control happen at every level. Their keys are theirs. Their decisions are logged.
**After graduation (with Juno):** Full transparency into trust bonds, peer connections, infrastructure.
**Competitive position:** "Trust, not convenience."

### Developers / AI Practitioners
**Alice shows them:** Code ownership, modification, deployment. The entity is theirs to shape.
**After graduation (with Juno):** Build on it. Extend it. Peer with other builders.
**Competitive position:** "Ownership, not lock-in."

### Enterprise / Business
**Alice shows them:** Governance on your hardware. No vendor changes your terms.
**After graduation (with Juno):** Optionality about peering. Run solo or peer — your choice.
**Competitive position:** "Independence, not dependence."

### Open Source Community
**Alice shows them:** Code is open. Decisions are auditable. It's a commons, not a platform.
**After graduation (with Juno):** Build a shared ring of sovereign entities.
**Competitive position:** "Collective sovereignty, not enclosure."
